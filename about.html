From a humanist ...

I completed my Ph.D. in Comparative Literature at Purdue University. As a humanist researcher, I was intereted in how and why medicine-related topics were represented or misrepresented in cultural products, such as literature and movies, and how these (mis)representations shaped the public's understanding about various concepts like illnesses, ability/disability, race, gender, and class. I also worked at Purdue as an instructor of record, teaching Chinese and Chinese literature at various levels.

To a humanist coder ...

I did not make my mind to change my career path until I took a course called Digital Humanities, where I learned how technology were used to accelerate research in humanities, a field which traditionally seemed to have nothing to do with technology. Amazed by how technological frameworks and methods made complex analysis and data-mining possible, I wanted to learn more about software development. The completion of a doctoral degree is a process of finding and solving problems, and I would like to keep searching and providing solutions in a fast-changing technological world, which is full of challenging and interesting tasks.
